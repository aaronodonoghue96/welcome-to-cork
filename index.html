Cork is a city of...

History

Cork is a city with a rich history, with its first settlement dating back to the sixth century, where it was founded by St Finbarr as a monastic site. Cork has many historical sites to offer, such as castles in Blarney and Blackrock, and Barryscourt Castle near Carrigtwohill, and other historical sites like St Finbarr's Cathedral and the Cork Butter Museum, which was at one point the world's largest butter market.

Culture

Cork is also known for its unique culture, and variety of events in the realm of the arts, such as film, theatre, and music. For theatre, Cork Opera House and the Everyman Palace offer a whole host of shows. The Cork Film Festival takes place every November, showcasing the works of talented filmmakers across Cork, and venues such as Páirc Uí Caoimh and the Marquee are known for holding concerts.

Entertainment

Cork is a great place for those looking to enjoy themselves, as in addition to the above mentioned events, there are also many other places in Cork to go for entertainment. Bowling, pool, laser tag, escape rooms, outdoor activities, all available in Cork, and a unique fusion of a retro arcade with a restaurant and bar, known as Barcadia, located in the Mardyke.

Cuisine

Cork is home to a variety of restaurants, with over 20 national cuisines represented. From well-known cuisines like Italian, Indian and Chinese, to some more unique options like Nepalese, Palestinian and Venezuelan, there's something for every palate in Cork. If drinks are more your thing than food, Cork also offers a wide range of pubs and bars, and the Jameson distillery in Midleton might be your thing.
